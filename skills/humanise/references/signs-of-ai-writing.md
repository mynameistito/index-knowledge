# Signs of AI-Sounding Writing

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Signs_of_AI_writing

Use these patterns as writing-quality heuristics, not authorship proof.

## Caveat

AI detectors and human intuition can be unreliable. Frame findings as "AI-sounding patterns" and focus on improving the draft.

## Content Patterns

- Inflated significance: excessive emphasis on legacy, importance, impact, or broad trends.
- Canned notability: formulaic claims about recognition, media coverage, or attribution without useful detail.
- Superficial analysis: polished summary that says little beyond obvious background.
- Promotional tone: ad-like praise, stacked adjectives, and unearned superlatives.
- Vague attribution: "many believe", "critics argue", "experts say", or broad opinion claims without support.
- Formulaic endings: generic discussion of challenges, opportunities, future prospects, or broader implications.

## Language Patterns

- High-density AI vocabulary: overused words such as "delve", "underscore", "realm", "intricate", "pivotal", "notable", "robust", "seamless", "transformative", and "testament".
- Avoidance of plain "is" or "are" phrasing in favor of inflated constructions.
- Negative parallelisms: repeated "not just X but Y", "not X but Y", or "rather than" structures.
- Rule of three: too many neat three-item lists or triads.
- Elegant variation: avoiding repeated simple terms so aggressively that the prose becomes unnatural.

## Style And Formatting Patterns

- Unnecessary title case, boldface, vertical lists, or tables.
- Heavy em dash usage when commas or periods would be more natural.
- Curly quotes or unusual typography pasted into plain-text contexts.
- Markdown, broken wiki markup, placeholder citations, fake references, or search-result links leaking into prose.

## Conversation Patterns

- Canned assurance about quality, good faith, collaboration, or policy adherence.
- Overly diplomatic offers to accept constructive criticism.
- Knowledge-cutoff disclaimers or speculation about source gaps when the user did not ask.
- Template-like subject lines, section titles, or placeholder text.

## Humanizing Moves

- Replace broad claims with concrete details already present in the draft.
- Cut inflated stakes unless the draft earns them.
- Use plainer verbs and nouns.
- Break symmetrical sentence patterns.
- Let the writer's actual stance show: preference, uncertainty, irritation, enthusiasm, care, or practical intent.
- Use contractions and sentence fragments only where they match the desired voice.
- Keep useful specificity even when it makes the prose less perfectly balanced.
